# Style Bible

## Visual Target

Stylized-realistic educational infographic: readable enough for students,
organic enough to avoid toy-like or default primitive models, and optimized
enough for Roblox.

Reference images are review baselines, not geometry templates. Use them to
preserve recognizability, component relationships, color language, and quality
bar, then translate those cues into organic 3D form. See
`REFERENCE_IMAGE_DESIGN_PRINCIPLES.md`.

## Shape Language

- Rounded, soft, biological silhouettes.
- Asymmetry and nonuniform spacing.
- Layered membranes where biology requires thickness.
- Surface detail from bevels, bumps, ridges, pores, vesicles, folds, and subtle
  color variation.
- Clear component hierarchy from far, medium, and close camera distances.

## Material Language

- Use color to separate function, not to decorate arbitrarily.
- Favor translucent membranes, softly glossy organelle surfaces, and subtle
  procedural texture.
- Avoid single-hue scenes. Each organelle needs a dominant color plus secondary
  biological accents.

## Rejection Rules

Reject assets that look like:

- plain spheres, cylinders, toruses, or cubes
- flat diagrams extruded into 3D without organic finish
- random decorative bubbles
- unreadable noisy clusters
- high-detail meshes that cannot survive Roblox import constraints
- flat copies of reference images without 3D biological thickness, asymmetry,
  or material variation
